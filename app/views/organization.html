Board of Directors

Tommie Cervetti

Board Chair

Tommie Cervetti has served on the ITNMemphis Board since 2011 and was elected Secretary in January, 2013. She is retired from Shelby County Government, having served 12 years as Manager of the County Mayor's Office on Aging then Manager for Information and Assistance for the Aging Commission of the Mid South. She served two terms as President of the Council on Aging, and was Chair of the Advisory Council for the Senior Companion Advisory Council at MIFA.

Cervetti received a Presidential Appointment to the 1995 White House Conference on Aging and also served as Chair of the Board of Catholic Charities, President of her Parish Council. She holds a BSE from the University of Arkansas and an MA in Urban Anthropology from the University of Memphis.

Reverend Dr. Mark Matheny

Founding Board Member

Reverend Doctor Mark Matheny has been involved with ITNMemphis since 2010 and has served on the board since 2011. Matheny retired as the Senior Minister at St. Luke’s United Methodist Church in Memphis. Dr. Matheny now works with several other non-profits in the community, such as Highland Area Renewal Corporation (HARC) and United Methodist Neighborhood Centers of Memphis.

A native of Hattiesburg, MS, Rev. Matheny is a third-generation United Methodist minister who received degrees from Millsaps College, Southern Methodist University and Emory University.

Pat Hickman

Acting Operations Manager

Pat Hickman has served on the Board of ITNMemphis since its inception in 2010. She also serves as an officer for the Highland Area Renewal Corporation (HARC), which served as the fiduciary agent for Friends of ITNMemphis before 501 (c) 3 status and affiliation with ITNAmerica was secured. She volunteered to be the Acting Operations Manager in July 2014 to carry the program to its first ride in December 2014.

In October 2016 she received the ITNAmerica Volunteer of the Year Award. She is an elder at Buntyn Presbyterian Church where ITNMemphis offices are located. Hickman hold a BS in Nursing from Vanderbilt University and an MS in Education from the University of Memphis.

Caroline Sweeney

Board Member

Caroline "Vickie" Sweeney is retired from Baptist Memorial Hospital and is currently a rider with ITNMemphis. She is a graduate of Kingsbury High School. She is an avid Memphis Grizzlies and University of Memphis fan.

Michael Bradshaw

Board Member

Michael J. Bradshaw, Jr. is an attorney with Butler Snow Law firm in Memphis as a member of the Public Finance, Tax Incentives and Credit Markets group. He is admitted to the Bar in Tennessee, Georgia and the District of Columbia.

Rev. Chris Carter

Advisory Board

Reverend Chris Carter joined the Advisory Board in 2016 after serving 3 years on the Board of Directors as Congregational Liaison with Christ United Methodist Church. Dan Murrell

Chair

Dan H. Murrell, CFRE, is the founding board president of ITNMemphis where he served until 2013. His interest in senior transportation issues stems from his time as Director of Development and Community Relations at Trezevant Manor, a continuing care retirement community in Memphis, TN. He and friend, Fred Wimmer, recognized the need for such a service and set out to bring an affiliate of ITNAmerica to Memphis.

Murrell has been in nonprofit development work since 1997 and is now Director of Planned Giving at the University of Memphis. He earned the CFRE accreditation in 2008, the only internationally-recognized baseline professional credential for philanthropic fundraising executives.
